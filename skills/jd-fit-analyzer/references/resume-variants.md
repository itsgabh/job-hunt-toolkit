# Resume Variant Selection Guide

This toolkit assumes four resume variants (kept in `cv-bullet-tailor/references/original-resumes/`). The JD fit analyzer recommends which to use for each role based on the JD's framing. Fill the variants in with your own content; the positioning hooks below are prompts for what each variant should foreground, not facts about any specific person. The JD fit analyzer should recommend which to use for each role based on the JD's framing.

---

## Variant 1: Master Resume

**Positioning**: General [your field] professional / senior individual contributor with full work history and tool stack visible. The neutral default.

**Use when**:
- The JD is a clean role squarely in your field at a tech/SaaS/remote-first company
- The role is squarely within your current track (similar scope and remit)
- You aren't sure which variant to recommend and want a safe default

**Don't use when**:
- A more specific variant would resonate more (Specialist-IC for senior IC, Non-profit for mission-driven, Consulting for fractional)

**What to foreground** (fill from your profile):
- Total years and breadth (e.g. "N+ years global remote-first")
- Any AI / automation angle you genuinely have
- Core systems / migration experience in your field
- Leadership exposure (formal or informal)

---

## Variant 2: Specialist-IC Resume

**Positioning**: Senior individual contributor / specialist in your field with deep hands-on and systems expertise. More tightly focused on hands-on, full-cycle execution work.

**Use when**:
- JD is for a **senior specialist** or similar individual contributor role in your field
- JD emphasizes hands-on ownership: end-to-end execution, systems work, automation, documentation
- The hiring company is a scale-up looking for an experienced IC, not a strategic lead
- The role does NOT require formal people management

**Don't use when**:
- The role expects strategy ownership or executive partnering as primary scope (use Master or Non-profit framing)
- The role is fractional / advisory / part-time (use Consulting)
- The company is mission-driven and that's the angle (use Non-profit)

**What to foreground** (fill from your profile):
- Your hands-on IC scope (team size, what you solely own)
- Population and geography supported (e.g. "N people across M countries")
- A concrete systems migration you led
- A scale story (growth you supported)
- Any team-lead or interim-lead experience

---

## Variant 3: Non-profit Resume

**Positioning**: [your field] professional with deep NGO leadership grounding, framed as mission-driven and cross-cultural.

**Use when**:
- The hiring org is a non-profit, NGO, B-corp, foundation, civic-tech, climate-tech, ed-tech with strong mission framing, social-impact org
- The JD emphasizes mission alignment, cross-cultural work, community-building, or values-driven culture
- The role is at a purpose-driven for-profit where the mission is genuinely central (not just marketing copy)

**Don't use when**:
- Standard commercial SaaS / enterprise software with no clear mission angle (use Master or Specialist-IC)
- The non-profit angle would feel forced

**What to foreground** (fill from your profile):
- Any mission-driven or NGO leadership grounding you have
- Cross-cultural / multi-country experience
- Mentorship and values-driven leadership framing
- A line on wanting roles where your work amplifies impact

---

## Variant 4: Consulting Resume

**Positioning**: [your field] consultant available for project, fractional, or advisory work. Make any self-employed / contractor authorization explicit if you have it.

**Use when**:
- The role is explicitly fractional, advisory, contract, project-based, or part-time
- The JD comes from a fractional-talent platform or freelance marketplace
- A founder is asking for help building foundations in your field, not for a full-time hire
- The role allows or expects external consulting alongside

**Don't use when**:
- Standard full-time employment role (use Master, Specialist-IC, or Non-profit)

**What to foreground** (fill from your profile):
- "How I help" service categories (e.g. [your service categories])
- Availability for project / fractional / advisory engagements
- Your location and invoicing / self-employed status, if relevant
- A brief intro-call CTA

---

## Decision tree

Use in this order, picking the first that matches:

1. Is the engagement type fractional / advisory / contract / part-time? → **Consulting**
2. Is the company explicitly mission-driven (non-profit, NGO, impact-for-profit)? → **Non-profit**
3. Is the role a senior IC role in your field at a scale-up? → **Specialist-IC**
4. Otherwise → **Master**

If two variants seem equally plausible, prefer the one whose positioning hooks map more directly to the JD's most prominent themes (read the first paragraph of the JD's "what you'll do" and the first three bullets of "what we're looking for").

---

## Tailoring priorities (regardless of variant)

When recommending a variant, also suggest the top three tailoring priorities for the specific JD:

1. Reorder the summary bullets so the top 2 align with the JD's most-emphasized themes
2. Lift specific tools/systems the JD names into the skills line where you have them
3. Front-load any role-specific scope match (population, geography, programs) in the most relevant work history bullet
