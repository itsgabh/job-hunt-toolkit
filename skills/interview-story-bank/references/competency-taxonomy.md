# Competency Taxonomy

The competencies stories are tagged against, organized by theme. Each competency has a one-line definition and 2–3 common interview question phrasings. Use this to map an interview question to a competency, then search `seed-stories.md` for matching stories.

A given story usually carries one **primary** competency tag plus 1–3 **secondary** tags. The primary is the angle the story is best for; secondaries cover related questions where the same story still works.

---

## Leadership and management

### Leading without authority
Influencing or aligning people who don't report to you.
- "Tell me about a time you led a team without formal authority."
- "Describe a time you had to get buy-in from people across functions."
- "When have you driven change without being the official decision-maker?"

### First-time management / informal team lead
Operating as the de facto lead before holding the title, or stepping into management for the first time.
- "Have you ever managed people? What was that like?"
- "Tell me about a time you stepped into a leadership role unexpectedly."

### Mentorship and growing others
Coaching, developing, or sponsoring colleagues.
- "Tell me about a time you helped someone grow."
- "Who's the person you've coached most directly, and what did you do?"

### Managing up
Influencing leadership, advocating for resources, pushing back on a manager.
- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager."
- "How do you bring concerns to senior leaders?"

### Cross-functional leadership
Coordinating across departments without a shared reporting line.
- "Describe a cross-functional project you led."
- "Tell me about a time you had to align engineering, product, and ops."

## Ambiguity and prioritization

### Working under ambiguity
Operating without clear scope, instructions, or success metrics.
- "Tell me about a time you had to make progress without a clear plan."
- "When have you started something without a defined endpoint?"

### Conflicting priorities
Handling multiple urgent things at once.
- "Tell me about a time you had too many priorities."
- "How do you decide what to drop when everything is on fire?"

### Saying no / protecting focus
Declining work, scoping down, pushing back on requests.
- "Tell me about a time you said no to a request."
- "How do you protect your team from scope creep?"

### Making decisions with incomplete info
Calling shots under uncertainty.
- "Tell me about a tough call you had to make with limited data."

## Conflict and disagreement

### Disagreement with a peer
Resolving substantive differences with a colleague at your level.
- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a coworker."
- "Describe a conflict and how you resolved it."

### Disagreement with a manager
Pushing back, advocating for a different approach with your manager.
- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your boss."

### Difficult feedback (giving)
Delivering hard news, performance feedback, or course corrections.
- "Tell me about a time you had to give difficult feedback."

### Difficult feedback (receiving)
Receiving hard feedback and acting on it.
- "Tell me about a time you got feedback that was hard to hear."

### Resolving team conflict
Mediating or untangling friction within a team.
- "Have you ever had to resolve a conflict between two colleagues?"

---

## Execution and ownership

### End-to-end ownership of a project
Carrying something from idea to launch.
- "Tell me about a project you owned end to end."
- "Walk me through something you delivered from scratch."

### Recovering from a setback
A project failed, slipped, or hit a wall — and what you did next.
- "Tell me about a time something didn't go as planned."
- "Describe a failure and what you learned."

### Scope-stretch / new domain
Taking on work outside your existing expertise.
- "Tell me about a time you had to learn something new fast."
- "When have you stepped into a domain you didn't know?"

### Hitting a deadline under pressure
Delivering when time was tight.
- "Tell me about a time you had to deliver under pressure."

---

## Change and adaptability

### Driving change
Leading a meaningful shift in how things work.
- "Tell me about a change you led."
- "How have you influenced a team's way of working?"

### Adapting to organizational change
Operating well through reorgs, layoffs, leadership changes, mergers.
- "Tell me about a time the company changed direction."

### Tool / process migrations
System migrations specifically (a core platform in your field, work-management tooling).
- "Tell me about a system migration you led."
- "Walk me through a tool implementation."

### Async / remote / distributed work
Operating in async-first or fully-remote environments.
- "How do you work async?"
- "Tell me about a time you collaborated across time zones."

## Cross-domain depth

These competencies show up across most fields. Tag stories against them wherever they apply.

### AI / automation adoption
Applying AI or automation to traditionally manual work.
- "Tell me about a time you brought AI into your work."
- "Where have you replaced manual work with automation?"

### Multi-country / multi-cultural operations
Operating across geos with different norms and laws.
- "Tell me about a time you ran something across multiple countries."

### Data and analytics
Using data to drive decisions in your function.
- "How do you use data in your work?"

### Scaling a function
Growing your function alongside company growth.
- "How have you supported your team during scaling?"

## Domain-specific competencies (example: People/HR — swap for your field)

A close call on a hire or a no-hire.
- "Tell me about a hiring decision you regret."
- "Describe a time you had to advocate for or against a candidate."

### Underperformer conversation
A performance issue and how it was handled.
- "Tell me about a time you addressed underperformance."

### Compensation / promotion conversation
Tricky comp or promotion discussions.
- "Have you ever had to deny someone a promotion?"

Building or rebuilding onboarding.
- "Walk me through an onboarding program you've designed."

Writing or revising policies.
- "Tell me about a policy you wrote or rewrote."

A migration of a system specific to the field (for People/HR: an HRIS or ATS).

## Tag conventions

When tagging a story:

1. **One primary tag** — the competency the story is the strongest example of
2. **1–3 secondary tags** — related competencies the same story can support
3. **Avoid over-tagging.** If a story claims to be the go-to for more than four competencies, it's probably too generic to be sharp on any one of them.
4. **Use the taxonomy as-is.** If a story doesn't fit any existing competency, consider adding a new one — but only after confirming it's genuinely missing, not a near-duplicate of an existing tag.
